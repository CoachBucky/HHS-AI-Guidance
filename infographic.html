AI in Our Schools

A Parent's Guide to Artificial Intelligence

Your child is growing up in an AI-powered world. We're committed to teaching them how to use AI tools safely, responsibly, and effectively while preserving the human connection that makes learning meaningful.

ü§ñ What is AI?

Think of AI like a very smart computer assistant that can help with writing, research, and creative projects. Just like calculators help with math, AI tools can help with many school tasks - but students still need to learn, think critically, and do their own work.

Experiment with ChatGPT or Google's Gemini to understand what your child is learning about. This helps you have informed conversations about AI use.

üí¨ Keep Talking

Ask your child how they're using AI for school projects. Make sure they understand teacher expectations and when AI use is appropriate.

Just like screen time limits, establish when and how AI can be used for homework. Encourage critical thinking over shortcuts.

üìö Emphasize Learning

Remind your child that AI should help them learn and understand, not do their thinking for them. The goal is to become smarter, not more dependent.

üìù What Students CAN Do

AI as a Learning Helper

  • Get help brainstorming creative ideas for projects
  • Practice language skills with AI tutoring
  • Generate study guides and practice quizzes
  • Ask for explanations of difficult concepts
  • Get feedback on draft writing (with teacher approval)
  • Research assistance and finding sources

üö´ What Students CANNOT Do

These Actions Are Not Allowed

  • Copy AI responses without citing the source
  • Submit AI work as their own original thinking
  • Use AI for tests or quizzes (unless allowed)
  • Create inappropriate or harmful content
  • Use AI to bully or impersonate others
  • Rely on AI without checking if it's correct
